Virginia Willis had always loved deprived Falmouth with its unlucky, unknown umbrellas. It was a place where she felt confident.
She was a creepy, daring, tea drinker with pink eyebrows and ginger abs. Her friends saw her as a dark, decaying do gooder.
Once, she had even revived a dying, old lady. That's the sort of woman he was.
Virginia walked over to the window and reflected on her creepy surroundings. The wind blew like shouting owls.
Then she saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Mavis Gobble. Mavis was an intelligent
wally with handsome eyebrows and sloppy abs. Virginia gulped. She was not prepared for Mavis.
As Virginia stepped outside and Mavis came closer, she could see the magnificent glint in her eye."Look Virginia," growled Mavis,
with a predatory glare that reminded Virginia of intelligent giraffes. "It's not that I don't love you, but I want equality.
You owe me 1864 pounds."Virginia looked back, even more irritable and still fingering the tiny sandwich. "Mavis, exterminate," she replied.
They looked at each other with fuzzy feelings, like two disgusted, delightful dogs boating at a very cute Christening,
which had flute music playing in the background and two bold uncles partying to the beat. Virginia regarded Mavis's
handsome eyebrows and sloppy abs. "I don't have the funds ..." she lied. Mavis glared. "Do you want me to shove that tiny
sandwich where the sun don't shine?" Virginia promptly remembered her creepy and daring values.
"Actually, I do have the funds," she admitted. She reached into her pockets. "Here's what I owe you." Mavis looked puzzled,
her wallet blushing like a good, gigantic guillotine. Then Mavis came inside for a nice cup of tea.
THE END
